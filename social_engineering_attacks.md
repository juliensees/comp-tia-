Social Engineering -attacks using psychological tricks to manipulate people into performing an action or giving info
  - Successful Social Engineering Attack Strategies
      - Authority - people defer to authority 
      - Intimidation - scaring people with threats if they don't comply 
      - Consensus - the herd mentality
      - Scarcity - making people believe that if they don't act quickly, they'll miss the opportunity
      - Urgency - the attacker creates a situation that time is running out (need access immediately!)
      - Familiarity - people say yes to someone they like
   
  Impersonation Attacks
    - Phishing - tricking useres into revealing passwords/info
      - usually randomly sent to many people from all over
          - IS SUCCESSFUL EVEN IF IT NETS A SINGLE VICTIM!!
    - Spear Phishing - targeted attacks (specific employees of a business)
        - Invoice scams are common spear phishing attacks
    - Whaling Attacks - focus specifically on senior executives
        - common tactic is to send fake court documents to senior business leaders, saying that their org is being             sued and must click link to read legal paperwork
    - Pharming - begin with phishing, but go to farther lengths, like creating fake websites to capture info
    - Vishing - voice phishing attacks
    - Smishing and SPIM - SMS and IM spam messaging 
      - Spoofing - faking an identity of someone else when sending a message
      
  - Brand impersonation - attacks that use an org's identity against third parties

Identity Fraud
  - Pre-texting - an attacker contacts a third party company pretending to be the consumer and tries gaining access

Watering Hole Attacks - use sneaky techniques to lure unsuspecting users and infect their system
    - attacker has a specific victim in mind
    - attacker compromises a trusted website of their victim (that they know they consistently visit) and injects         malicious code into it, and uses drive-by-download to infect the victim silently 
  - Client Side Attacks - target a user and their device (NOT A SERVER)
        - exploit human behavior, instead of technical vulnerabilities
  - Drive-by Download - just visiting a malicious website from clicking a link

Physical social engineering
  - Shoulder Surfing - watching someone's screen over the shoulder
  - Dumpster Diving - digging through an org's trash
  - Tailgaiting - slipping in beside someone (holding a door for someone)

Business e-mail Compromise - BEC - targets businesses and individuals by impersonating a business or executive into transferring funds or sensitive info
  - Spoofing - attackers manipulate e-mail settings to appear as a trusted source
      - sometimes they actually compromise the account through a takeover
   
Misinformation - spread unknowningly
Disinformation - is disseminated with deliberate intent to deceive 
  - propoganda is a classic form
  - deepfakes
